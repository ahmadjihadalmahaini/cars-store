The Bavarian Motor Company, commonly referred to as BMW (German: [ˈbe: ˈʔɛmˈve:] (hear)), is a German multinational company that produces cars and motorcycles. The company was founded in 1916 as a manufacturer of aircraft engines, which produced from 1917 through 1918 and again from 1933 to 1945. Cars are marketed under the BMW, Mini and Rolls-Royce brands, and motorcycles are marketed under the BMW Motorrad brand. It is one of the list of competitors in cars in the world and is the biggest competitor to other very popular German cars such as Mercedes, Audi, Volkswagen, Maybach and Smart. In 2015, BMW was the second largest car producer in the world, with 2,279,503 vehicles produced. BMW is headquartered in Munich and produces cars in Germany, Brazil, China, India, South Africa, the United Kingdom, the United States and Mexico. The company has a proven track record in motorsports, particularly in Touring cars, Formula 1, sports cars and the Isle of Man TT. The company's logo is inspired by an airplane propeller, the German flag, and blue and white are the main colors of the Bavaria region, as the company's headquarters are in Munich in the state of Bavaria. The four-cylinder engine is an important part of BMW's legacy. These engines were present in most economic BMW cars, so the company's headquarters was designed in the form of a four-cylinder engine.
